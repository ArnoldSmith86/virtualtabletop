Add game

Upload VTT or PCIO files you downloaded from the web.

You can find compatible game files on a boardgamegeek.com geeklist, the PlayingCardsIO subreddit, its notion.so database or GitHub.

Please note that PCIO files might not be 100% compatible. Please open an issue on GitHub if a file is unplayable.

If you have a direct link to a VTT or PCIO file (for example by sharing a link from virtualtabletop.io), you can add the game using the link and you will always play the latest version.

You can also enter a Tabletop Simulator workshop link in order to import the widgets from it, but the imported game will not have any automation.

About VirtualTabletop.io

This project is licensed under GPLv3 and is available on GitHub.


Come join our discord server.
Check out our wiki page for helpful information about how to make your own games.

Online Gameplay

This is an open-source, free-to-use platform, with no accounts and no ads.

Anyone that knows the URL of the room that you are using may come in to the room at any time and take any action, including playing or changing games in the room or deleting games. Keep a personal, offline backup of everything that you care about.

Know who you are playing with. The platform is unmoderated and no one controls what may happen. Anything you do or share is your own responsibility.

Cheating is trivially easy, unpreventable, and undetectable. Just play for fun.

If you are under the age of majority in the location you are from, be sure to comply with the rules for accessing online material that apply to you.

Privacy Policy

VirtualTabletop.io does not ask for or collect any personal identification information.

VirtualTabletop.io does not use cookies.

VirtualTabletop.io does, however, collect the "player name" as well as a few other game-related settings, and keeps that data in local storage on the user's device. The player name defaults to a number, but is customizable to any user entered data. VirtualTabletop.io does not analyze or use that information except to display it on the screen while accessing the website.

Card Decks

• Standard & Themed Decks

Default card deck: Created by Adrian Kennard and released under CC0 at https://www.me.uk/cards/, including the jokers 3J and 4J. Jokers 1J and 2J are based on on Byron Knoll's "Playing cards set", which the author released into the public domain. Tsan-Kuang Lee made some adjustments: new layout to match others cards, improved visibility when cards are stacked with a narrow offset, and converted the font to SVG paths for better compability. The new works are also released into the public domain. Back 3B is based on the image from David Bellot's SVG cards, with a slight modification in sizing. The original work is licensed under GNU Lesser General Public License version 2.1. The modified version is licensed under GNU Lesser General Public License version 3.

Plastic card deck: Created by Gifford Cheung, licensed under CC BY 4.0. The deck uses the Comfortaa font, copyright Johan Aakerlunch (aajohan@gmail.com), licensed under the SIL Open Font License, Version 1.1. The license and FAQ are available at http://scripts.sil.org/OFL.

Skinny card deck: Created by an anonymous contributor who provided the work to VirtualTabletop.io with an unrestricted license. VirtualTabletop.io releases this deck into the public domain under CC0.

• Regional & Historical Decks

German suited card deck: The images and arrangements used in this deck are by Mfrasca and are available under CC BY-SA 3.0. The cards are derived from work by Gunter Gerhardt available under CC BY-SA 2.0. The arrangement of the 5 and 6 of each suit is not contained in the work by Mfrasca but was derived from that work by LawDawg96 and is released under CC BY-SA 3.0.

Spanish card deck: The images used in this deck are by Germarquezm and are available under CC BY-SA 3.0. The completed deck arrangement is by Basquetteur and is available under CC BY-SA 3.0.

Swiss suited card deck: The arrangements (and many of the images) are based on the German deck by Mfrasca and are available under CC BY-SA 3.0. The cards are derived from work by Gunter Gerhardt under CC BY-SA 2.0. The arrangement of the 6 and banner card of each suit was derived by LawDawg96 and is released under CC BY-SA 3.0. The flower is by Kilom691 under the GNU Free Documentation License v1.2 at Wikimedia Commons. The shield is by Kilom691 under CC-SA 3.0 at Wikimedia Commons. The flag is in the public domain under CC0 at freesvg.org.

• Tarot Decks

Tarot deck: The cavalier (knight) face cards were generated using the Midjourney artificial intelligence image generator, https://www.midjourney.com, using the default deck’s Jack of Clubs image as a starting point with the prompt: “on horseback line art style --iw 2 --v 5”. Released into the public domain under CC0.
